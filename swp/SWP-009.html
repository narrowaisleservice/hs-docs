Changing/ fitting a mast are usually one-man jobs. But do not hesitate to ask for assistance. Be knowledgeable about high pressure hydraulics equipment which moves the mast up and down. Never disconnect hydraulic line or fitting without first dropping pressure to zero. A high pressure oil stream can pierce the body and cause severe injuries. Use lifting equipment. Use weight rated slings. Read the label.

📝 Sequence of Work
Flexi Mast Installation
1
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
2
Ensure the battery is fitted to the truck.
3
Ensure mast support is positioned as if the forks are in front of the truck.
4
Apply the handbrake and chock the rear wheels.
5
Ensure the tilt cylinder rods are extended and rod ends are vertical.
6
Disconnect the battery.
7
Attach an appropriately weight-rated lifting sling for the weight of the mast around the top cross members of the mast and ensure that the sling is padded with suitable sheathing against sharp edges, Picture 1.
8
Lift the mast to the vertical position with a crane; (if lifting by fork lift truck, see note below). Ensure the mast is just clear off the ground and is not raised above head height, Picture 2.
9
Ensure the grease nipple hole in the bush aligns with the bush nipple, Picture 3.
10
Run a bar through the pivot holes. This ensures that when you manoeuvre the mast onto the mast support, it will not swing right inside. You need to create room so that you can reach through with your hands and access the hoses for fitting/ making connections, Picture 4.
11
Put plastic magnetic strips on the mast supports. This protects the mast support from scratching, and prevents any damage to paint work, Picture 5.
12
Manoeuvre the mast up to the mast support, Picture 6.
13
Raise and position the mast pivot shafts into the half round holes in the mast support, at the same time view the alignment of the tilt cylinder rose bearing with the mast lugs, Picture 7.
14
Note: Always keep some tension on the sling to control mast movement.
15
Fit the pivot shaft retaining clamps (also called caps) with cap head screws/ bolts. Put the left and right retaining clamps/ caps in the correct order. Secure the pivot-end plates, Picture 8.
16
Use small wooden blocks to support the tilt cylinder rose bearings, Picture 9.
17
With the mast tilted forward fit the tilt pins into the tilt cylinder rose bearings and mast lugs, Picture 10.
18
Connect the main lift hose, side shift hoses, and electrical cables as required.
19
Release the tension on the sling and use access equipment to remove it from the mast
20
Connect the battery and test functionality
21
Fit forks and stop bolts.
22
Note: Alternative method, i.e. If lifting mast using fork lift truck;
23
The forklift must be suitable to lift the weight of the mast and operated by qualified driver.
24
A special lifting beam must be attached to the forks to carry the mast, Picture 11.
25
The total lift height of the forklift must be 2m more than the closed height of the Flexi mast.
26
Consideration must be given to a swinging load and all manoeuvring - travelling must be smooth and in creep speed.
27
With a 2 man operation ensure communication and instructions are clear.
