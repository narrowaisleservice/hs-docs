Consider weight, size and shape of hydraulic steer motor. Wear safety shoes. Use gloves. Avoid contact with used hydraulic oil. Avoid pinching/ trapping. Handle the hydraulic steer motor safely. Use manual handling techniques acquired during manual handling training.

📝 Sequence of Work
Removal and Refitting of Hydraulic Steer Motor-Pinion Housing
1
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
2
Isolate the battery by releasing the battery plug sited on the right hand side of the panel below the hydraulic lever controls.
3
Remove the nose cone cover.
4
Remove the two steer hoses and bleed pipe, Picture 1 and Picture 2. (Warning: Take care when removing the steering hoses a small amount of pressure will be released, always cap the hoses and plug the steer motor to prevent contamination and leakage!).
5
bleed pipe connection
6
Remove the four M12 hex head setscrews securing the hydraulic steer motor –pinion housing to the chassis, Picture 3.
7
Lift the hydraulic steer motor-pinion housing clear of the chassis. Lift the hydraulic motor by hand. If the motor will not part from pinion housing, the complete housing unit will have to be removed and then separated on the work bench.
8
Caution: It is safe for one operative to lift the hydraulic steer motor-pinion housing when removing from the truck. But do not hesitate to ask for assistance.
9
Refitting
10
Refitting is the reversal of above.
11
Note: When lowering the hydraulic steer motor-pinion housing into the chassis take care that the pinion is in line with the slew bearing gear.
