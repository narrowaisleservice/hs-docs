Consider weight, size and shape of overhead guard. Wear safety shoes. Use gloves. Avoid contact with used hydraulic oil. Avoid pinching/ trapping. Handle the hydraulic steer motor safely. Use manual handling techniques acquired during manual handling training.

📝 Sequence of Work
Removal and Refitting of the Overhead Guard
1
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
2
Remove both side ballast weights as described in section SWP 015 Side Castings Removal/ Refitting.
3
Disconnect wiring connecting plugs and move clear of the chassis.
4
Attach suitable lifting device and slings to the overhead guard.
5
Take the strain of the overhead guard remove eight M14 Durlock bolts securing the overhead guard to the chassis.
6
Lift the overhead guard clear of the chassis taking care not to damage any of the attached wiring and plugs.
7
Refitting
8
Using a suitable lifting device and slings attached to the overhead guard raise the overhead guard above the chassis, slowly onto the chassis until the lip on the overhead guard sits firmly on the edge of the chassis, Picture 1.
9
Using the eight Durlock bolts secure the overhead guard to the chassis tightening to a torque of 256Nm.
10
Reconnect wiring plugs and refit the side ballast weights as described in section SWP 015 Side Castings Removal/ Refitting.
