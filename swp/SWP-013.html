Do not allow hydrogen gas to collect in the air. Charging of electric storage batteries gives off flammable hydrogen gas. This gas, when mixed with air, in the presence of a source of heat such as an electrical spark, will explode, causing the battery to explode as well. Ensure batteries are always handled in well ventilated areas (The workshop area is normally ventilated to acceptable levels). Only trained persons to put batteries on charge. Know how to check the water monitor/ check water level. Do not spill acid that is inside batteries when moving batteries. If accidental battery fluid spillage occurs, report it to supervisor or production manager straight away.
⚠ Hazard Analysis
Explosion
Hydrogen is easily ignited and explosive
Acid burns to face, hands and eyes

📝 Sequence of Work
Battery Removal/ Replacement
1
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
2
Disconnect Battery Isolator Plug sited on the right hand side of the panel below the hydraulic lever controls.
3
Remove battery lid.
4
Disconnect the flying lead (truck connection) from the battery.
5
Lift seat pan.
6
Lift battery stabilizing foot/ retaining latch which is on the battery container, Picture 1.
7
Choose a forklift truck or a specially adapted hand pallet truck with a rated capacity capable of carrying the weight of the battery, use short forks as a preference. (see rating plate on battery container), Picture 2.
