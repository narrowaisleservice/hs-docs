📝 Sequence of Work
Removal and Refitting of The Hydraulic Tank/ Oil Tank
1
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
2
Isolate the battery by disconnecting the battery plug sited on the right side of the panel below the hydraulic lever controls.
3
Remove the battery lid raise the seat pan.
4
Lean the electrical panel & spool valve forward to gain access to the hydraulic tank.
5
Remove the steer motor pump assembly and the lift motor pump assemblies as described in SWP 019. (Note: Variation to procedure: Work instructions may require you to remove oil tank with electric steer motor and lift motor still attached).
6
Drain the hydraulic fluid from the tank using a suction pump, Picture 1.
7
Note for field service repair procedures undertaken outside workshop;
8
Removing the hydraulic tank is normally a workshop/ manufacturing procedure (e.g. for rebuild or refurbished trucks). On rare occasions it has to be done at a customer’s site as a field service repair procedure in cases where there is a leak in the tank/ crack in the weld. In that kind of situation the suction pump may not be available to remove/ empty oil from the hydraulic tank. Raising the mast to full height helps removes some oil from the tank. However, the oil in the mast is then held by the check valve, and any mistakes could lead to oil flowing out on the floor. One has to be aware of which hoses not to disconnect (see hydraulic circuit).
9
Bear in mind also that the SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work requires you to lower the forks and the mast and to disable all forklift electrics. Raising the mast during operations is not recommended practice.
10
Remove the handbrake cable assembly and move clear of the hydraulic tank.
11
Warning: Take care when removing hoses (a small amount of pressure may be released).
12
Tip: To prevent contamination & component damage always plug or cap open fittings.
13
Remove hand brake fittings and associated brackets/ mechanism to avoid catching when lifting the hydraulic tank out, Picture 2.
14
Remove any remaining hoses and move clear of the hydraulic tank.
15
Remove cooling fans on VNA and Euro forklift trucks, Picture 3.
