📝 Sequence of Work
Removal and Refitting of The Electric Lift Motor Pump Assembly (or Removal and Refitting of Steer Motor Pump Assembly)
1
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
2
Isolate the battery by disconnecting the battery plug sited on the right side of the panel below the hydraulic lever controls.
3
Remove the battery lid raise the seat pan.
4
Remove the front electrical cover.
5
Remove five M10 hex head setscrews retaining the electrical panel & spool valve to the chassis.
6
Lean the electrical panel & spool valve forward to gain access to the lift motor (or the steer motor depending on procedure).
7
Remove the lift motor cables & auxiliary wiring (or the steer motor cables and auxiliary wiring depending on procedure).
8
Warning: Take care when removing hoses, a small amount of pressure may be released. Also, to prevent contamination & component damage, always plug or cap open fittings!
9
Remove all hoses shown in Picture 1 in the picture of a lift motor and move them clear of the tank assembly.
10
hoses
11
Remove the Breather attached to the Filter-Filler so as not to damage it when removing the pump-unit-securing plate.
12
Note: On Flexi Euro trucks remove the Filter-Filler housing completely to facilitate removal of the motor-pump unit.
13
Remove six M6 hex head setscrews on the plate securing the lift motor to the hydraulic tank. (Or remove six M6 hex head setscrews on the plate securing the steer motor to the hydraulic tank, depending on procedure). A picture of the plate securing the lift/ steer motor to the hydraulic tank is shown in Picture 2.
14
Attach the lifting jig to the lift motor (or the steer motor depending on procedure) using a fork truck or suitable lifting apparatus. Raise the lift/steer motor pump assemblies clear off the truck taking care not to damage the suction filter and pipes.
15
See picture of lift motor and pump in Picture 3. (Note: Pump removal from motor is separate procedure).

18
Refit the steer/ lift motor-pump assemblies in reverse order of above taking care not to damage the suction filter and pipes when lowering into the hydraulic tank.
