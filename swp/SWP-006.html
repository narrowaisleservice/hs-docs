📝 Sequence of Work
Drive Axle Removal/ Replacement
1
Field Service Repair Procedure (outside manufacturing workshop where overhead crane is not available and lifting operations are carried out using the forks of another forklift truck):
2
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work. Warning: The battery must NOT be removed from the truck during removal of the drive axle; the stability of the truck will be greatly reduced otherwise.
3
Apply the parking brake and chock the rear wheels.
4
Remove the forks (refer to SWP 11 Forks/ Fork Arms Removal or Replacement for procedure).
5
Remove the mast (refer to SWP 09 Flexi Mast Installation for procedure).
6
Note: If the mast is not of a very large size and therefore too heavy to compromise chassis stability, it can be left on the truck during the procedure. But always remove the forks to allow easy access to axle during this procedure.
7
Isolate the battery by releasing the battery plug sited on the right hand side of the panel below the hydraulic lever controls.
8
Tip: Loosen the drive wheel nuts if you need to remove the wheels after the axle is removed from the truck.
9
Open the counter weight, Picture 1. Note: check hinge weld/ pin condition for damage before opening counter weight, and be cautious as the counterweight door may drop.
10
Jack up the truck and block to gain access to the cross member/ axle support bar. Place two 5 ton jacks beneath the main chassis member where the step is attached to the chassis. DO NOT USE THE STEP AS A JACKING POINT. Raise the drive wheels clear of the floor high enough to allow access with an Allen key and then lower the chassis onto the FLT block system (Resin blocks) leaving the jack as a secondary support.
11
Loosen the two M20 Allen head cap screws securing the cross member, Picture 2. Do not remove the cap screws just yet.
12
Lower truck back down so that front tyres are in contact with the floor.
13
Remove 4 M20 hex head set pins holding axle from underneath.
14
Jack forklift chassis up off the axle and block with hardwood blocks.
15
Position the forks of another forklift truck to support the axle assembly only.
16
Now remove the two M20 Allen head cap screws securing the cross member to the axle.
17
Remove the two axle clamps. The axle assembly will be free to drop off from the forklift, supported by the forks off another forklift truck.
18
Use the other forklift truck to lower the assembly free of the chassis fabrication and back away to withdraw the assembly.
19
Workshop/ Manufacturing Procedure (when overhead crane is available):
20
Position the forklift truck/ chassis to be worked on in a spacious fitting bay.
21
Remove the forks (refer to SWP 11 Forks/ Fork Arms Removal or Replacement for procedure).
22
Remove the mast (refer to SWP 09 Flexi Mast Installation for procedure).
23
Attach crane on the main chassis member using appropriate weight rated sling to support the forklift chassis, Picture 3. Note: If truck is masted, use at least 5 tonne rated sling.
