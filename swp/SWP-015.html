Consider weight, size and shape of side castings. Wear safety shoes. Use gloves. Avoid contact with used hydraulic oil. Avoid pinching/ trapping. Handle the hydraulic steer motor safely. Use manual handling techniques acquired during manual handling training.

📝 Sequence of Work
Removal and Refitting of Side Ballast Weights
1
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
2
Note: It is good practice to remove the mast before removing the side-ballast weights.
3
Note: The stability of a chassis with a mast attached but without side ballast weights and without a battery is greatly compromised.
4
Remove the seat pan assembly as described in SWP 17 Seat Assembly Removal/ Fitting.
5
Using suitable lifting apparatus attach the lifting jig to the side ballast weight to be removed and take the strain.
6
Remove the three plastic cover inserts.
7
Remove and discard the three M20 socket head cap screws and spring washers (see note below).
8
Lift the side ballast weight clear of the truck, Picture 1.
9
Refitting the side ballast weight is the reversal of the above.
10
Note: Use new M20 socket head cap screws and spring washers apply Loctite Threadlock 242 adhesive before tightening when refitting the ballast weight.
