Changing/ fitting a mast are usually one-man jobs. But do not hesitate to ask for assistance. Be knowledgeable about high pressure hydraulics equipment which moves the mast up and down. Never disconnect hydraulic line or fitting without first dropping pressure to zero. A high pressure oil stream can pierce the body and cause severe injuries. Use lifting equipment. Use weight rated slings. Read the label.

📝 Sequence of Work
Flexi Mast Removal
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
1
Ensure the battery is fitted to the truck.
2
Apply the handbrake and chock the rear wheels
3
Ensure mast support is positioned in the straight-ahead position.
4
Remove the forks.
5
Ensure the tilt cylinders are extended.
6
Disconnect the battery.
7
Attach an appropriately weight-rated lifting sling for the weight of the mast around the top cross members of the mast and ensure that the sling is padded with suitable sheathing against sharp edges. (See Picture 1).
8
Raise the crane hook or fork lift attachment so that the mast is supported. (See picture 2)
9
Place a drip tray beneath the base of the mast and ensure the fork carriage is fully lowered. Undo the main lift hose, side shift, tilt hoses, mast micro switch connection and bleed pipe (see picture 3, 4 and 5)
10
Remove the retaining clips and tilt pins. (See picture 6).
11
Remove the retaining clamps (also called caps) with cap head screws/ bolts. Put the left and right retaining clamps/ caps in the correct order. (See picture 6)
12
Prepare a safe area to place the mast onto two suitable pallets. Raise the mast and lower onto the pallets. (See picture 7)
Note: Alternative method, i.e. If lifting mast using forklift truck;
13
The forklift must be suitable to lift the weight of the mast and operated by qualified driver.
14
A special lifting beam must be attached to the forks to carry the mast. (See Picture 8).
15
The total lift height of the forklift must be 2m more than the closed height of the Flexi mast.
16
Consideration must be given to a swinging load and all manoeuvring - travelling must be smooth and in creep speed.
17
With a 2 man operation ensure communication and instructions are clear.
Variation to Procedure
18
The Himax mast arrangement is slightly different where-by it’s bolted to the mast support at the bottom with 4 – 16 mm x 65mm long cap head setscrews, instead of pins through the tilt cylinder.
19
The side shift rigid pipes near the base are fitted with 2 flying hoses to assist with pipe connection when the mast is bolted in place.
20
Himax masts are paired with the mast support and are fitted with shims to provide the correct amount of lean back.
