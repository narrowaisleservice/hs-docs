Tilting a mast is usually a one-man job. But do not hesitate to ask for assistance. Be knowledgeable about high pressure hydraulics equipment which moves the mast up and down. Never disconnect hydraulic line or fitting without first dropping pressure to zero. A high-pressure oil stream can pierce the body and cause severe injuries. Use lifting equipment. Use weight rated slings. Read the label.

📝 Sequence of Work
Tilting Flexi Mast
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
1
Ensure the battery is fitted to the truck.
2
Apply the handbrake and chock the rear wheels.
3
Ensure mast support is positioned in the straight-ahead position.
4
Remove the forks.
5
Ensure the tilt cylinders are extended.
6
Disconnect the battery.
7
Attach an appropriately weight-rated lifting sling for the weight of the mast around the top cross members of the mast and ensure that the sling is padded with suitable sheathing against sharp edges. (See Picture 1).
8
Raise the crane hook or forklift attachment so that the mast is supported. (See picture 2)
9
Place a drip tray beneath the base of the mast and ensure the fork carriage is fully lowered. Undo the main lift hose, side shift, tilt hoses, mast micro switch connection and bleed pipe (see picture 3, 4 and 5). Fit blanking plugs into hydraulic hoses.
10
Remove the retaining clips and tilt pins. (See picture 6).
11
Using a crane slowing tilt mast back while maintaining a safe working distance. Fit tilt frame to chassis using long bolts. Tie down eyes must be fitted with nylon washers to protect paintwork.
12
Secure pipe work and wiring as required. (see picture 7)
13
When tilt frame is mounted to chassis lift frame to secure to pin holes on mast. (See picture 8)
Note: Alternative method, i.e. If lifting mast using forklift truck.
14
The forklift must be suitable to lift the weight of the mast and operated by qualified driver.
15
A special lifting beam must be attached to the forks to carry the mast. (See Picture 9).
16
The total lift height of the forklift must be 2m more than the closed height of the Flexi mast.
17
Consideration must be given to a swinging load and all manoeuvring - travelling must be smooth and in creep speed.
18
With a 2-man operation ensure communication and instructions are clear.
Variation to Procedure
19
The Himax mast arrangement is slightly different where-by its bolted to the mast support at the bottom with 4 – 16 mm x 65mm long cap head setscrews, instead of pins through the tilt cylinder.
20
The side shift rigid pipes near the base are fitted with 2 flying hoses to assist with pipe connection when the mast is bolted in place.
21
Himax masts are paired with the mast support and are fitted with shims to provide the correct amount of lean back.
