📝 Sequence of Work
Loading Flexi Trucks onto Lorry Trailer
Note: Only qualified/competent operators who have attended a Flexi Truck licence should carry out the following procedure. For all overseas deliveries, it is the responsibility of Narrow Aisle to safely load the containers and ADVISE the driver where to secure the load.
1
Ensure the trailer brakes are on and where necessary wheels are chocked on the ramp and fit ramp chains to lorry.
2
Ensure driver works under supervision of the qualified/competent operators and when not required, the driver must wait in a safe place until required to strap down/secure load.
3
Ensure the ramp is lowered sufficiently to truck bed. (See Picture 1)
4
Align the Flexi in the straight ahead position with the middle of the ramp. Do not attempt to drive on the edge of the ramp. (See Picture 2)
5
Drive up the ramp and onto the trailer.
6
Position the Flexi, apply the handbrake lower forks and tilt forward.
7
Chock the wheels and secure the Flexi truck with ratchet straps as appropriate. (See Picture 3)
8
To offload from the trailer is the reverse of the above.
NOTE: The Flexi is a pivot steer truck and therefore has different manoeuvring characteristics to a conventional counter balance FLT.
9
.
10
An unladen Flexi can be loaded driving forks first (preferable) or fork trailing.
11
A Laden truck i.e. with charger or spare battery must be loaded driving up the ramp with forks forwards
Warning: Batteries have a high centre of gravity and must be handled with care and extreme caution. Wherever possible load battery onto trailer first using a suitable counter balance truck
12
Do not attempt to turn the truck on the trailer slope.
13
Observe mast overall height for transit or tilt back mast to travel is an option
14
Ensure pictures are taken to confirm load has been secured safely
