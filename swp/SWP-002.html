📝 Sequence of Work
Removal Of The Front Drive Wheel
1
Ensure the battery remains in the truck when there is a mast on the truck to maintain chassis stability.
2
Apply the handbrake and chock the rear wheels.
3
Raise the forks approximately 100mm off the floor slide both forks to the centre of the carriage and point forward of the truck. Lower to the ground.
4
Isolate the battery by disconnecting the battery plug sited on the right side of the panel below the hydraulic lever controls. The counter weight door must be fully closed and secure with the M20 cap head retaining setscrew. The assembly is pivoted in the centre and articulates up and down.
5
Loosen the six wheel nuts on the wheel to be removed.
6
Place a toe jack under the front counter weight door on the side wheel to be removed, Picture 1.
7
Raise the wheel just clear of the floor. Remember the new wheel is of a greater diameter that old, worn out wheel being removed.
8
Place the FLT block system (Resin blocks) next to the jack and lower the truck onto the support leaving the jack in place as a secondary support system. Do not put body parts under truck.
9
Remove the six wheel nuts
10
Remove the wheel from the axle. The wheel is heavy, so do not lift the wheel. Slide the yellow wheel dolly under the wheel. Lower the wheel onto the dolly. Roll the wheel and dolly away clear from the fitting bay.
11
When replacing the wheel ease the wheel onto the wheel studs using the special tool shown in Picture 2.
12
When the wheel is on the floor, the wheel nuts must be tightened to a torque setting of 290(N-m)
13
as specified in the Flexi Workshop Manual.
