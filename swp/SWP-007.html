📝 Sequence of Work
Slewing Bearing Removal/ Installation
1
Field Service Repair Procedure (outside manufacturing workshop where overhead crane is not available and lifting operations are carried out using the forks of another forklift truck):
2
Refer to SWP 001 Before Starting Work/ Putting Forklift Truck in Safe Position before carrying out the following work.
3
Note: Keep battery in the truck during removal of the mast support & axle assembly to maintain chassis stability.
4
Isolate the battery by releasing the battery plug sited on the right hand side of the panel below the hydraulic lever controls.
5
Remove the forks (refer to SWP 11 Forks/ Fork Arms Removal or Replacement for procedure).
6
Remove the mast (refer to SWP 09 Flexi Mast Installation for procedure).
7
Remove the nose cone cover.
8
Remove the M20 cap head setscrew retaining the counter weight door, Picture 1.
9
Note: Check hinge weld/ pin condition for damage before opening counter weight, and be cautious as the counterweight door may drop to the ground.
10
Fully open the counter weight door to gain access to the drive motor cables, Picture 2.
11
Note: The drive motor shown in the picture is an AC motor. The older version of trucks will have a DC motor.
12
Disconnect the motor cables and auxiliary plug.
13
Pull the drive motor cables & auxiliary plug clear of the mast support & chassis.
14
Remove the two hoses from the left hand & right hand tilt cylinders. Plug and cap the hoses.
15
Remove all mast hoses. Plug and cap the hoses.
16
Remove the two steering motor hoses & extension housing.
17
Close and secure the rear door.
18
Remove 23 M12 hex head setscrews & washers securing the slewing bearing to the chassis.
19
Position the forks of another fork truck with a rated capacity capable of carrying the weight of the mast support & axle assembly between the top of the tilt cylinders and the mast support.
20
Raise the drive wheels clear of the floor by placing two jacks underneath the main chassis member where the step’s attached to the chassis. DO NOT USE THE STEP AS A JACKING POINT. Jack up forklift chassis clear from the mast support & axle assembly.
21
The mast support & axle assembly should now be only supported by the forks of another forklift truck. Drive the supporting forklift backwards and draw the assembly away from the front of the forklift chassis.
22
The slewing bearing is attached to the mast support. Remove the 24 M12 hex head setscrews & washers which attach the slewing bearing to the mast support, Picture 3.
23
Note: All bolts & washers securing the slewing bearing to the mast support & chassis must be replaced & tightened to a torque of 120Nm.
24
Refitting the slewing bearing is the reversal of the above instructions.
25
Workshop/ Manufacturing Safe Working Practice (when overhead crane is available):
26
Position the forklift chassis from which you want to remove the slewing bearing in a spacious fitting bay.
27
Isolate the battery by releasing the battery plug sited on the right hand side of the panel below the hydraulic lever controls.
28
Remove the forks (refer to SWP 11 Forks/ Fork Arms Removal or Replacement for procedure).
29
Remove the mast (refer to SWP 09 Flexi Mast Installation for procedure).
30
Fully open the counter weight door to gain access to the drive motor cables.
31
Disconnect the motor cables and auxiliary plug.
32
Pull the drive motor cables & auxiliary plug clear off the mast support & chassis.
33
Remove the two hoses from the left hand & right hand tilt cylinders. Plug and cap the hoses.
34
Remove all mast hoses. Plug and cap the hoses.
35
Remove the two steering motor hoses & extension housing.
36
Attach an overhead crane on the main chassis member using appropriate weight rated sling, Picture 4.
37
Note: Alternatively you can insert lifting eye-bolts on the forklift chassis lift eye-bolt holes situated on the nose of the truck. Then put a sling through the lifting eye-bolts and attach crane to sling to take the weight of the chassis.
38
Place a hand pallet truck/ pump truck under the mast support and axle assembly, ready to support the weight of the mast support and axle assembly.
39
Remove 23 M12 hex head setscrews & washers securing the slewing bearing to the chassis.
40
Lift the chassis high enough to raise it and clear the mast support & axle assembly using an overhead crane.
41
Draw away the mast support and axle assembly using a pump truck/ hand pallet truck.
42
The slewing bearing is attached to the mast support. Remove the 24 M12 hex head setscrews & washers which attach the slewing bearing to the mast support.
