Inhalation of airborne dust from brake components etc Short term and long-term respiratory disorders E 4 2 8

  • There are good levels of natural ventilation throughout the premises.

  • Compressed air is not used to blow down or blow dust.

  • Unit is well ventilated with doors open wherever possible.

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Ensure vacuums are used to clear up dust/debris.
Injury to eyes Minor to serious injury due to entry of foreign particles into the eyes E 4 3 12

  • There are good levels of natural ventilation throughout the premises.

  • Suitable safety glasses are supplied and worn when using compressed air equipment.

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Ensure that swarf/dust residues are vacuumed as opposed to blown with air to reduce the generation of airborne dust.
Injection of compressed air into bloodstream Severe injury/ fatality E 5 2 10

  • Compressed air IS NOT used for blowing individuals down or blowing in areas where employees may be working.

  • Compressed air is used at the lowest pressure practical.

  • Compressed air lines and equipment is checked before use.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Ensure that swarf/dust residues are vacuumed as opposed to blown with air to reduce the generation of airborne dust.
Compressor / equipment Failure Serious injury/ fatality due to fire or explosion E 5 3 15

  • Compressed air is used at lowest pressure.

  • The Air receiver/ compressed air tank is located outside, in the compressor house, away from workshop operations to reduce risk of explosion. This also reduces noise.

  • Compressor is serviced and subject to 12 monthly Thorough Examination under a written scheme of inspection. Records held on site.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Ensure pipe work and attachments are inspected regularly, include in weekly inspection regime and record details.
