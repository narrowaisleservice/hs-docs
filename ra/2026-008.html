Manual handling of material Muscle strain, cuts and abrasions, E 4 3 12

  • Machine only accepts small pieces, by design, limiting weight

  • 2nd worker to assist is material is of significant weight or size

  • Bed is at ergonomic height, by design, to optimise handling tasks

  • Gloves worn by workers when handling material

  • Safety footwear worn by workers

  • Training in handling techniques delivered to workers

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Continue to supervise workers to ensure safe working practice is followed
Slips, trips and falls around machine Cuts, bruises, head injuries A 3 3 9

  • Reasonable lighting levels throughout workplace

  • Cables are routed overhead or behind machine to reduce trip hazards

  • Safety footwear worn by workers

  • Quarterly H&S meeting and inspection of work areas

 3 1 3 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing controls.
Contact with electricity Electrocution E 5 2 10

  • Fixed electrical systems in buildings are routinely inspected for condition

  • Electrical cables are mostly shrouded in casing of the machine, by design, limiting access

  • Workers check cabling before using machine

  • Cables are routed overhead or behind machine to reduce risk of damage

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing testing regimes
Exposure to noise generated by process Hearing damage E 4 3 12

  • Hearing protection plugs or defenders worn by workers

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Continue to supervise workers to ensure safe working practice is followed
Entanglement within exposed moving parts Crush injury or scalping E 4 2 8

  • Material is positioned by the employee.

  • The drill head has a guard to prevent contact with the moving parts.

 4 2 8 M

  • Continue to supervise workers to ensure safe working practice is followed and guard remains in place.

  • Use a workpiece clamp where possible.

  • DO NOT use gloves whilst operating.

  • Ensure telescopic guard is fully engaged.
Cutting by exposed part Cut/crush injury E 4 2 8

  • Material is positioned by the worker and clamped into position.

  • The drill head has a guard to prevent contact with the moving parts.

  • Control handle physically removes one hand from cutting process

 4 2 8 M

  • Continue to supervise workers to ensure safe working practice is followed and guard remains in place.

  • Use a workpiece clamp where possible.

  • DO NOT use gloves whilst operating.

  • Ensure telescopic guard is fully engaged.
Flying material ejected by equipment Eye injury E 4 3 12

  • Machine has guard which should prevent the ejection of particles hitting the worker with any force.

  • Guard position ensures chuck key is removed

  • Eye protection worn by workers

  • Eye protection PPE signage displayed near machine

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Continue to supervise workers to ensure safe working practice is followed.
Poor maintenance of equipment, leading to uncontrolled movement or exaggerated emissions Major injury resulting from any of the above hazards A 5 3 15

  • Weekly recorded inspection of machine
