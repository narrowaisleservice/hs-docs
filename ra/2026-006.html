Failure of cranes Failure could be fatal and cause crane or load to fall and crush persons A 5 2 10

  • Crane is serviced in accordance with manufacturers’ recommendations

  • Crane is subject to Thorough Examination by competent engineer.

  • Safe Working Load (SWL) is clearly marked and never exceeded.

  • All workers are made aware of Safe Working Load.

  • Lifting plans are developed for all lifting operations.

  • All lifting operations are undertaken by trained and competent persons

  • Maintain existing controls
Poor maintenance and inspection of crane Falls from height, electrocution etc. A Maintain existing controls
Lifting operations Failure of equipment, loads falling etc. A 5 2 10

  • Crane and lifting equipment is subject to Thorough Examinations by Competent Person.

  • Only trained and competent persons undertake lifting operations.

  • Hard hats/bump caps used for any lifting operation.

  • Report any damaged equipment to Supervisor – never use defective equipment

  • Only use inspected and tested lifting equipment.

  • Work area is clear from trucks, pedestrians and other obstructions.

  • No lifting into blind areas or over pedestrians.

  • Safety footwear worn by workers.

  • Workers receive training in loading and slinging by an external training provider periodically.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Continue to supervise workers to ensure safe working practice is followed
Collison with raised masts in factory. Death, crushing etc. A 5 4 20

  • Lifts are properly planned and lifting plans produced.

  • Pendant is bagged and locked to prevent overhead crane being used when masts are extended in bays.

  • Bag is only removed when masts are lowered to safe height for overhead crane to operate

  • Pedestrians are warned of lifting activity in their area.

  • NEVER stand or walk below a load.

  • Safety footwear worn by workers.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Continue to supervise workers to ensure safe working practice is followed.

  • Deliver toolbox talk to workers who operate overhead crane.
