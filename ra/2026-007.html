Manual handling of material Muscle strain, cuts and abrasions, E 4 3 12

  • Size of grinding wheel limits the size of the piece, minimising weight

  • 2nd worker to assist if material is of awkward shape or size

  • Safety footwear worn by workers

  • Maintain existing control measures
Slips, trips and falls around machine Cuts, bruises, head injuries A 3 3 9

  • Vacuums used to clean workplace on routine basis

  • Reasonable lighting levels throughout workplace

  • Cables are routed behind machine to reduce trip hazards

  • Safety footwear worn by workers

  • Quarterly H&S meetings and inspections of workplace in place to monitor and manage workplace conditions

 3 1 3 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing control measures
Contact with electricity Electrocution E 5 2 10

  • Fixed electrical systems in buildings are routinely inspected for condition

  • Electrical cables are mostly shrouded in casing of the machine, by design, limiting access

  • Workers check cabling before using machine

  • Cables are routed overhead or behind machine to reduce risk of damage

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing testing regimes
Exposure to noise generated by process Hearing damage E 4 3 12

  • Hearing protection PPE signage displayed near machine

  • Hearing protection plugs or defenders worn by workers

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing control measures
Exposure to vibration generated by process Hand arm vibration syndrome E 4 3 12

  • Short duration work limits exposure

  • Infrequent use of machine limits exposure

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing control measures
Entanglement within exposed moving parts Crush injury or scalping E 4 2 8

  • Moving parts are shrouded, as far as possible

  • Rotating parts are smooth to reduce risk, by design

  • Piece held against wheel, physically between wheel and operator

  • Fixed moveable guards in place on machine

 4 2 8 M

  • Supervise workers to ensure safe working practice is followed

  • Use a workpiece clamp wherever possible.

Poor maintenance of equipment, leading to uncontrolled movement or exaggerated emissions Major injury resulting from any of the above hazards A 5 3 15

  • Maintenance / repair work undertaken by competent persons only
