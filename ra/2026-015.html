Heat source / flame Burn injuries, splashes and spatter from hot materials E 4 3 12

  • PPE suitable to a hot work environment is supplied and worn during welding activities – gauntlets and aprons.

  • All PPE is regularly condition checked and maintained in good order.

  • Welding equipment is operated by competent persons only.

  • All equipment is subject to visual inspections prior to commencing work including hoses, connectors, flashback arrestors etc.

  • Ensure all welding equipment is subject to annual servicing and inspection by a Competent Person.

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing control measures.
Fire / explosion Death, damage to property A 5 3 15

  • Welding equipment is operated by competent persons only.

  • All welding activities are undertaken in dedicated welding bays only.

  • Welding bays are protected with suitable welding partitions.

  • All equipment is subject to visual inspections prior to commencing work including hoses, connectors, flashback arrestors etc.

  • Welding equipment is regularly serviced.

  • All compressed gas cylinders are stored in small quantity and in well-ventilated area away from any sources of ignition.

  • Gas cylinders are chained to the wall or suitable trolley away from the working area.

  • Suitable PPE is supplied and worn in this work area e.g. overalls, gloves, visor

  • Operative ensures that all hot work has been allowed sufficient time to cool before leaving the premises.

  • The welding bays are kept free from combustible materials to reduce the risk of fire incident.

  • Fire marshals received training in fire safety and safe use of extinguishers.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing controls
Welding fume Respiratory ill health due to inhalation of welding fume. E 4 4 16

  • Welding bay is equipped with portable captor Local Exhaust Ventilation which is serviced and Thoroughly Examined at 14 monthly intervals by a competent third party.

  • Records are kept of the inspections undertaken.

  • There is good general ventilation

  • Lung function health monitoring regime is in place for all welding and paint shop workers

  • COSHH assessment has been undertaken for the welding booth work areas.

 4 3 12 M

  • Issue powered air filter hoods to welding operatives

  • Carry out a suitable air monitoring survey in the metal shop.
Damage to eyes and skin from UV Rays etc. Damage to eyes E 3 4 12

  • PPE provided and used in the welding area to reduce the risk of eye injury – welding visors.

  • Welding partitioning provided and used to protect other operatives in production area.

  • Signage at the entrance to the welding bay highlighting risk and warning of welding activity taking place
