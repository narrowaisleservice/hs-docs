Contact with vehicles / other FLT’s / overhead obstructions etc. Crushing, death E 5 3 15

  • Engineers ensure that a suitable flat, clean area is provided by the customer where the truck can be serviced safely.

  • Engineers ensure that the area is away from plant and traffic movements and any other hazards (Racking, machinery, overhead hazards, noise etc.)

  • A sign is placed on steering wheel or seat highlighting the fact the truck is being worked on.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Carryout regular audits of Service engineers.
Use of hand tools Cuts, bruises etc E 3 2 6

  • Workers provided with their own basic hand tools at induction

  • Hand tools are visually inspected prior to use and any defective equipment is withdrawn from service.

  • Engineers are provided with suitable PPE including gloves, eye protection and protective footwear.

  • Hand tools are not left unattended at any time.

 3 2 6 M

  • Manager to carryout regular checks of engineer’s basic hand tools.
Trapping in moving parts etc. Cuts, bruising etc. E 5 3 15

  • Mast is lowered, hand brake applied and the truck isolated and key removed prior to undertaking work on truck.

  • Where necessary, wheels are chocked as additional measure to prevent truck moving.

  • Work is only carried out on flat, stable ground wherever possible.

  • If mast cannot be lowered, it must be suitably propped and supported with a suitably rated and fitted prop to prevent mast failing and crushing engineer.

  • Mast must only be raised for a minimum amount of time.

  • Safety helmet to be worn.

  • FLT is only jacked up the minimum amount to remove wheels.

  • Engineers instructed to never climb or crawl underneath a FLT.

  • See section on use of jacks.

 4 2 8 M

Use of power tools Entanglement, flying objects etc. E 4 3 12

  • Engineers are competent in the use of power tools.

  • Power tools are inspected, and PAT tested regularly.

  • All guards and safety devices are fitted to power tools and in working order.

  • Discs are correct size and type.

  • Drill bits are sharp and of correct size.

  • Extension leads only used when absolutely necessary, fully unwound and routed away from walkways.

  • Engineers wear suitable work clothing and PPE to include eye protection, ear protection and safety footwear.

Slips and Trips Broken bones, cuts, bruises E 3 2 6

  • Engineers wear a suitable safety boot or shoe with steel toe cap and non-slip sole.

  • Work area is clean and free from oils and greases etc.

  • Overspray from chain lube etc. is cleared to prevent slip hazard.

  • Engineers instructed to keep to designated walkways if available.

Hazardous Substances Skin, respiratory, eye contact and associated problems. A 3 3 9

  • Only the minimum amounts of products are carried.

  • All hazardous substances are individually assessed.

  • No substance can be brought into service without prior assessment by the company.

  • Work is not undertaken close to sources of ignition.

  • All substances are clearly marked in original containers.

  • Engineers are equipped with suitable impervious gloves, safety goggles and FFP3 respirators.

  • Fire extinguishers are available close to hand, along with a first aid kit and suitable eye washes.

  • COSHH risk assessments and MSDS’s reviewed regularly.

  • Engineers issued with COSHH assessments for the use of substances.

  • Annual skin assessments are undertaken by management.

  • Engineers are trained and competent to operate fork lift trucks.

  • Ensure site traffic management rules are adhered to.

Use of Jacks Crushing, failure of equipment etc. A 5 3 15

  • Jacks are subject to Thorough Examination every 12 months.

  • Jacks are visually inspected prior to use and any defects reported immediately.

  • Engineers trained to ensure that trucks are only lifted the absolute minimum to inspect the brakes.

  • Only the recognised jacking point is used.

  • Ensure that a secondary support is used (rated blocks).

  • Engineers instructed to never crawl or place any part of the body under the truck.

  • Jacks only used on solid, flat and stable ground.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing controls
Work at Height Falls from height, broken bones etc. E 5 3 15

  • Work is undertaken from the ground wherever possible.

  • Engineers are instructed NEVER to stand on the overhead guard or raised forks or any other structure such as racking, pallets etc.
