Explosion Hydrogen is easily ignited and explosive Explosion, fire, burns A 5 3 15

  • Battery charging instructions followed.

  • Batteries are always handled in well ventilated areas (The workshop area is normally ventilated to acceptable levels).

  • Visual inspection of charging plugs carried out.

  • Only trained authorised workers put batteries on charge and to monitor/ check water level.

  • Appropriate PPE worn during battery charging operations (Safety goggles and gloves)

  • Suitable fire extinguishers (dry powder or CO2) nearby.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing control measures.
Acid burns to face, hands and eyes Batteries contain sulphuric acid which is highly corrosive.
Chemical injuries or burns to skin, eyes, etc.		 E 4 3 12

  • Only trained authorised workers put batteries on charge and to check the water levels.

  • Appropriate PPE worn during battery charging operations (Safety goggles and gloves)

  • Wash hands after working with batteries, and before eating or smoking.

  • If acid contact or splashes are suspected, use plenty of water to clean affected body area.

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing control measures.
Electrical arcing Short circuit Burns to skin, hands, eyes/ shocks/ flash injuries. E 5 3 15

  • Batteries sufficiently electrically insulated.

  • When carrying out battery maintenance, insulated tools are used.

  • Switch off/ isolate forklift truck when charging a battery if it is in situ i.e. on a forklift truck.

  • Before charging begins, electrolyte levels are checked.

  • Manufacturer’s instructions on charging rates and ventilation adhered to.

  • Regular inspection of charging equipment for defects (Ensure electrical installation to charger and all leads are in good condition).

  • Battery chargers switched off or disconnected from power before charging leads are connected. Lead will be securely fixed before the charger is switched on, and the charger will be switched off before any disconnection.

  • Electrolyte levels checked after charging and topped up as necessary.

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing control measures.
