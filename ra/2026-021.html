Poor ventilation Ill health A 3 1 3

  • Good general ventilation

  • Welding bays and spray booths have local extraction to reduce airborne contaminants

  • Quarterly H&S meetings and inspections of all areas as part of a scheduled monitoring process

  • LEV is Thoroughly Examined by a competent person at least every 14 months

Extremes in temperature Discomfort, distraction A 3 3 9

  • Heating provided by fixed gas fuelled heaters, during cooler months

  • Roller shutter opened to ventilate in warmer months.

  • Gas appliances are serviced annually

 3 1 3 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing controls
Work Equipment Poor health, bacterial infection A 4 3 12

  • Workers provided with own basic hand tools at induction

  • All significant work equipment is purchased through the senior management team for approval.

  • Significant work equipment is maintained in accordance with manufacturers recommendations and where applicable inspected in line with current legislation, records are maintained and available for inspection.

  • Faulty items of equipment are reported to the works manager.

  • All workers are trained in significant work equipment and refresher training takes place. All training records are maintained and available for inspection.

  • Workers are instructed not to use significant work equipment unless they have been trained

  • For any work equipment that falls under LOLER (lifting equipment) refer to the specific risk assessments.

  • All work equipment complies to EU an UK standards and legislation and is fit for purpose.

  • Where fitted, safety guards must not be removed from any work equipment or work tooling. Where temporary repairs are undertaken all safety guards/covers/grilles must be replaced to prevent access to any moving parts.

 4 2 8 M

  • Ensure daily visual checks of work equipment is undertaken by workers and before each use.

  • Manager to carryout regular checks of workers basic hand tools.
Lack of PPE Cuts, burns fumes, infections, muscular injuries E 3 2 6

  • All Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is selected and maintained so that it is suitable for the purpose for which it is to be used.

  • A PPE purchasing process is in place.

  • PPE is inspected regularly by management and records held by the business. Defect reporting and replacement process is in place).

  • No charge is levied for the provision of PPE equipment.

  • PPE is always readily available for the workers.

  • Suitable storage facilities are provided so the PPE can be stored in a suitable manner by the workers.

  • Workers are provided with instructions and training to maintain PPE in an efficient state. (this includes replacing or cleaning as required).

  • Users are trained at induction in the correct use of the PPE.

 3 1 3 ✓ Acceptable

  • Maintain existing controls
Chemicals / Hazardous Substances A 4 3 12

  • All hazardous substances are individually assessed.

  • Workers are trained at induction training where to locate chemical information

  • Workers are provided with basic information in how to use hazardous chemicals safely.

  • No substance can be brought into service without prior assessment by the company.

  • COSHH information is readily available in the factory at all times (MSDS and risk assessments)

  • COSHH risk assessments and MSDS’s are reviewed annually.

  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is supplied.

  • Dedicated and segregated storage is in place for chemicals.

  • Occupational health screening in place

 4 2 8 M

  • Monitor and maintain existing controls
Diseases (Bacteria / viruses) A 3 3 9

  • The risk of infection from a biological source is minimal however, hand cleaning facilities are supplied.

  • Health surveillance programmes in place for welding and spraying activities.

  • Protective gloves are issued and used.

  • First-aid kits are supplied.

  • Overalls are supplied to reduce the risk of exposure to chemicals and oils

  • Maintain provision
