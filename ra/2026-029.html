Falls from Height Hop ups, steps etc. Death, broken bones, fractures etc. E 5 3 15

  • ALWAYS look to work from the ground where reasonably practical to do so.

  • Work planned to ensure that wherever practical work undertaken from the ground (assembly of components etc.).

  • Selection of access equipment based on site-specific assessment and safest reasonable option.

  • Steps are visually inspected prior to use.

  • Do not use defective steps.

  • Steps are only used for light work and short periods. (20 minutes/10kg guide)

  • Wherever possible use podiums or ‘hop-ups’ as opposed to steps.

  • Workers are wear suitable safety boots.

  • Good housekeeping is maintained on site.

  • Podium/hop up steps are only used on flat stable base.

 5 2 10 M

  • Only use access equipment where all other safer alternatives have been considered first.

  • Separate RA required for access equipment.

  • Reinforce with regular tool-box talks.
Falling Objects Head injuries, cuts etc. A 3 3 9

  • No objects such as tools and equipment are stored at height e.g. on buckets/machine steps etc.

  • tools kept in tool bag as opposed to carrying tools.

  • Personnel kept out of the working area.

  • Other employees/contractors aware of work.

  • Access equipment is not left unattended.

  • Warning signs displayed in the vicinity of the work area.

  • Monitor and maintain existing controls.
Use of Power tools (Drills, grinders, disc cutters, drills etc.) Entanglement, cuts etc. E 4 3 12

  • Tools supplied are suitable and adequate for the job.

  • Employees are trained and competent in the use of the equipment.

  • All equipment is visually checked prior to use and defects reported before use.

  • Only battery rechargeable or 110v equipment used.

  • All guards and safety devices are in place before use.

  • No loose clothing / jewellery / long hair whilst working.

  • Drill bits are sharp and of the correct size.

  • Grinders only used by competent people (Trained to change and dress wheels)

  • Abrasive wheels are suitable construction and speed for machine.

  • Any grinding undertaken is in a clear area away from flammable liquids/vapours and materials – obtain hot work permit.

  • Employees wear suitable safety boots, impact eye protection, ear defenders and an approved respirator – FFP3 standard.

  • See vibration, noise and asbestos categories.

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing controls.
Contact with Electricity Electric Shock burns E 5 3 15

  • All electrical tools and equipment are visually inspected prior to use and any defects reported prior to use.

  • Only battery rechargeable or 110v equipment used on site.

  • Workers are trained and competent.

  • Cables are clipped and routed overhead wherever practical.

  • Tools are PAT tested every 12 months

 5 1 5 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing controls.
Hand Arm Vibration from use of power tools HAVS E 4 2 8

  • Vibration damped equipment is specified wherever possible.

  • Vibration data is obtained for all equipment

  • Jobs using vibrating tools are rotated amongst the team.

  • Tools and equipment are well maintained and any defects reported immediately.

  • Operatives wear gloves.

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Undertake health surveillance.
Exposure to Noise from use of power tools Hearing loss, tinnitus etc. A 4 3 12

  • Noise dampened tools are specified wherever possible.

  • Ear defenders are formally issued and employees instructed to use them when using power tools.

  • Personnel are instructed of times when noisy activities will be undertaken.

  • Tools and equipment are well maintained and any defects reported immediately.

 4 1 4 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing controls.
Exposure to Dust etc. to workers and personnel, fume and dust etc Lung irritation, respiratory disease. E 3 3 9

  • Tools with extraction systems used where practical for prolonged dust generating activity (ideally 110v on-tool vacuum system as opposed to bags).

  • Suitable FFP 3 approved respirators are worn when generating dust.

  • Dust generated is vacuumed as opposed to swept.

  • Keep personnel out of the working area at all times.

 3 1 3 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing controls.

  • Issue powered air filter hoods to welding operatives
Hazardous Substances oils, grease, crack detector, acetylene etc. Skin, eye, lung irritation etc. A 3 3 9

  • Substances are kept in original containers in a suitable storage container on site.

  • Welding/cutting etc. only undertaken in well-ventilated areas.

  • COSHH Assessments and data sheets available for substances – refer to these documents.

 3 1 3 ✓ Acceptable

  • Ensure employees are taken through assessments and understand how to use substances.

  • Ensure suitable approved gloves, eye protection and respirators are available where required by COSHH assessment.

  • Ensure that only the minimum amount of substance is used and stored on site / in vehicles.
Manual Handling Back injuries, strains etc. E 3 4 12

  • Never attempt to move anything if it is beyond your capability.

  • Workers equipped with suitable PPE (Safety boots, gloves etc.)

  • Trolleys/sack trucks etc. are used wherever practical on site.

  • Site is assessed prior to arrival to identify a suitable parking area as close to the site as possible to reduce carrying distance.

  • Work area is cleared prior to work commencing to reduce risk of slips / trips etc.

  • Suitable, tidy storage area or flat area is available on site.

  • Employees are trained in manual handling techniques.

 3 1 3 ✓ Acceptable

  • Undertake separate manual handling assessments for installation work.
Slips and trips Cuts, bruises, broken bones etc. E 3 3 9

  • Walk around of work areas prior to unloading vehicle to identify suitable storage locations etc.

  • Points identified of access and egress prior to works commencing.

  • Works planned to limit the need to trail cables / block walkways etc.

  • Tools or equipment are not left in walkways etc.

  • Workers are equipped with suitable safety boots.

  • Work areas remain kept tidy

 3 1 3 ✓ Acceptable

  • Monitor and maintain existing controls.
